What does a scam message look like?

Spot the Scam: Common Examples

Scammers often use similar tricks. Look at these examples to learn what warning signs to watch for.

🏦

Bank Scam

Example message

"DBS: Your account has been suspended. Click this link now to verify your details."

Warning signs

  • Urgent warning
  • Suspicious link
  • Requests login details or OTP
👤

Singpass Scam

Example message

"Singpass Alert: Your account will be disabled today. Confirm your NRIC and OTP now."

Warning signs

  • Threat of account closure
  • Requests NRIC or OTP
  • Uses Singpass name to sound official
📦

Delivery Scam

Example message

"Your parcel is waiting. Pay $1.99 through this link to arrange delivery."

Warning signs

  • Unexpected parcel
  • Small payment request
  • Link to arrange delivery
💼

Job Scam

Example message

"Earn $300 a day from home. Pay a registration fee now to start your job."

Warning signs

  • Easy money promise
  • Asks for payment first
  • Little job information
📈

Investment Scam

Example message

"Our expert has a guaranteed investment plan. Transfer money today for high profits."

Warning signs

  • Guaranteed profit
  • Pressure to transfer money
  • Too good to be true
🏛

Government Scam

Example message

"Police Investigation: You are involved in a case. Call this number immediately."

Warning signs

  • Fear or legal threat
  • Urgent call-back request
  • Unexpected contact
💬

Fake Friend Scam

Example message

"Hi, I changed my number. Can you lend me money urgently? I will return it tomorrow."

Warning signs

  • New number claim
  • Urgent money request
  • Asks you not to call
💖

Love Scam

Example message

"I need help paying for an emergency. Please send money now. I promise to visit soon."

Warning signs

  • Fast emotional attachment
  • Emergency money request
  • Excuses to avoid meeting

Stop. Check. Ask someone you trust.
