What should I do to stay safe?

Scam Safety Guide

Use these simple steps whenever a message, call, or link makes you feel pressured or unsure.

Before you act

Pause and protect yourself

  • Stop before clicking. Do not rush because a message says it is urgent.
  • Never share private details. Keep OTPs, NRIC, Singpass passwords, bank details, and login passwords private.
  • Open official apps directly. Use your bank, Singpass, or delivery app instead of a link in a message.
  • Use official contact details. Find the organisation's hotline from its official website or app.
  • Ask someone you trust. A family member or friend can help you slow down and check.

When you are unsure

How to check if a message is genuine

🔍

Check the sender and link

Look carefully at phone numbers, email addresses, and website names. Small spelling changes can be a warning sign.

📞

Contact the organisation yourself

Call the official hotline or use the official app. Do not reply to the suspicious message.

If something has already happened

Act quickly, but stay calm

  • If you sent money, contact your bank immediately and ask what you can do next.
  • If you shared a password, change it through the official website or app right away.
  • If you shared an OTP, NRIC, or Singpass details, contact the relevant organisation through official channels.
  • Keep screenshots, phone numbers, and transaction details in case you need to make a report.
