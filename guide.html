Scam awareness

Scam Guide

Learn the common warning signs before clicking links, sharing details, or replying to unknown senders.

Warning Signs

Common scam red flags

⏰

Urgency and fear

Scammers often pressure you to act immediately so you do not have time to think.

🔗

Suspicious links

Links may look official but lead to fake websites that steal personal details.

🔐

OTP or password requests

Real organisations should not ask you to send OTPs or passwords through chat.

🏦

Singpass or bank impersonation

Messages may pretend to be from trusted services to make you lower your guard.

🎁

Too-good-to-be-true rewards

Unexpected prizes, rewards, or refunds can be used to trick users into clicking.

📦

Delivery scams

Fake parcel messages may ask you to pay a small fee or confirm personal details.

Examples

Example scam messages

Fake bank message

"Your account has been suspended. Click this link to verify your details immediately."

Fake Singpass alert

"Singpass login attempt detected. Send your OTP now to stop account closure."

Fake delivery message

"Your parcel cannot be delivered. Pay a small fee through this link to reschedule."

Fake prize message

"Congratulations, you won a reward. Login now and provide your bank details to claim."

Safe Actions

What to do when unsure

  • Do not click suspicious links.
  • Do not share OTP, password, NRIC, Singpass, or bank details.
  • Open official apps or websites directly.
  • Call the official hotline if you are unsure.
  • Ask a trusted family member before acting.
